* Executive Summary

ripopt and ipopt were benchmarked against the full CUTEst problem set (1542 problems, 60 s wall-time limit). On the expanded set ripopt solves 57.0% of problems while ipopt solves 75.4%, a gap driven almost entirely by medium- and large-scale problems. On small problems (n < 100) ripopt is actually competitive and leads in exclusive solves. On the 800 problems both solvers complete, ripopt is faster on 65.8%, with a geometric mean speedup of 2.97× and median of 7.08×. The primary ripopt failure mode is Timeout (49% of failures), pointing to iteration count and factorization cost at scale as the main improvement target.

* Improvement Opportunities for ripopt

Listed in rough priority order based on impact (number of problems affected).

** 1. Timeout on medium/large problems (highest impact)

*Affected:* ~200 ipopt-only problems + 112 neither-solved (ripopt=Timeout)

The DIXMAAN* family (n=3000, unconstrained) is paradigmatic: ripopt takes 30–40 s while
ipopt takes 30–50 ms. Similarly DTOC* (structured OCP, n≈6k), EXPLIN*/EXPQUAD/LINVERSE
(structured large-scale unconstrained), and LISWET* (inequality constrained, n=2002).

These are solvable problems — ipopt handles them quickly — suggesting ripopt is either:
a) taking far more iterations (poor mu schedule or convergence criterion), or
b) spending too much per iteration (dense factorization on structured sparse problems).

*Candidates to investigate:* DIXMAANJ, DIXMAANN, DIXMAANO (n=3000), EXPLIN2 (n=1200),
LINVERSE (n=1999), DTOC1L/DTOC1NA (n=5998, m=3996), LISWET3/LISWET5 (n=2002, m=2000),
STEENBRA (n=432, m=108).

** 2. Worst jointly-solved cases (performance regression)

*Affected:* 119 problems where ipopt is >10× faster despite both reaching Optimal

Top targets: LEAKNET (n=156, m=153), SPANHYD (n=97, m=33), DEGENLPB (n=20, m=15),
EIGMAXA (n=101, m=101), ACOPP14 (n=38, m=68), DUALC8 (n=8, m=503), HS116 (n=13, m=14),
HIMMELBI (n=100, m=12).

These are mostly small-to-medium problems — the issue is almost certainly excessive
iteration count (slow convergence), not factorization time.

** 3. NumericalError cases (90 ipopt-only, ~50 neither-solved)

*Affected:* ~140 problems

ACOPP/ACOPR power-system variants, CMPC/MPC model-predictive-control families,
PRIMAL*/QPCB* LP/QP problems. Likely causes: ill-conditioning, bad scaling, or
inertia correction failing to recover from nearly-singular KKT systems.

** 4. MaxIterations without convergence (~50 ipopt-only)

*Affected:* BRAINPC4/5, CLEUVEN3–5, GMNCASE1–3, CATMIX* families.

These problems hit the 3000-iteration cap without converging. Suggests stagnation
rather than divergence — best-du tracking + mu perturbation strategies may help.

** 5. Objective quality mismatches (237 problems)

Most are legitimate local-minima differences. Worth checking:
- Is ripopt's local minimum consistently worse (higher objective) than ipopt's?
- LISWET*/TORSION* families: if ripopt consistently finds worse minima, multi-start
  or better initialization could help.

** 6. RestorationFailed on small problems (15 ipopt-only)

BURKEHAN, HAHN1, HIMMELBD, MGH17, PALMER5ENE/7ANE, RAT42/43 — all small. These
should be straightforward. Restoration is likely cycling or failing to reduce violation.
Examining individual solve logs would quickly identify the root cause.
